ΔIf is a counterfactual archive.

It does not describe what is. It asks what would remain if something were missing — or what would collapse if it were excessive.

ΔIf bir karşı-olgusal arşivdir.

Olanı anlatmaz. Bir şey eksik olsaydı geriye ne kalacağını — ya da aşırı olsaydı neyin çökeceğini sorar.

Assumption of Absence

What disappears first is not the object, but the assumption that it was ever necessary. Silence does not answer questions; it simply removes the space where the question could stand. Without the structure we rely on, meaning does not vanish—it simply becomes fluid.

Assumption of Excess

Excess does not announce itself. It arrives disguised as comfort, repeating itself until the absence of noise feels like violence. When a signal is everywhere, it ceases to be information and becomes environment.

Yokluk Varsayımı

Aşırılık Varsayımı

Aşırılık kendini duyurarak gelmez. Konfor kılığında gelir, gürültünün yokluğu şiddet gibi hissedilene kadar kendini tekrar eder. Bir sinyal her yerde olduğunda, bilgi olmaktan çıkar ve ortama dönüşür.
