ΔIf

The One Who Assumed
What if it were missing?
What if it were excessive?

ΔIf is a counterfactual archive.

It does not describe what is. It asks what would remain if something were missing — or what would collapse if it were excessive.

Every text here assumes a condition and follows its consequences.

Assumption

What disappears first is not the object, but the assumption that it was ever necessary.

Excess does not announce itself. It arrives disguised as comfort, repeating itself until absence feels violent.
