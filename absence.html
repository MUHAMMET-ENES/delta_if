Assumption of Absence

What disappears first is not the object, but the assumption that it was ever necessary. Silence does not answer questions; it simply removes the space where the question could stand.

Without the structure we rely on, meaning does not vanish—it simply becomes fluid, terrifyingly free. We build walls not to keep things out, but to define what is "in". When the wall is gone, the definition of "here" evaporates.

This is the paradox of absence: it has a weight. It occupies space. The empty chair at the table is heavier than the occupied one.

— The One Who Assumed
